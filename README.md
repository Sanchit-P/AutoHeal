# Title: AutoHeal
## Hackathon Problem Statement – “Autonomous IT: Rethinking the Future of Self-Managing Systems” 
### Context 
Today, large-scale IT environments—whether in cloud, on-premise, or edge setups—are becoming too complex for manual management. With thousands of interconnected services, continuous deployments, and dynamic workloads, human operators struggle to maintain system reliability, security, and cost-efficiency. The next leap forward is Autonomous IT — intelligent systems capable of self-monitoring, self-healing, self-optimizing, and self-securing, inspired by human-like adaptive behavior. 
### Challenge 
Your task is to design and prototype an autonomous IT solution that can detect, decide, and act with minimal or no human intervention. The solution should demonstrate how automation combined with intelligence can improve reliability, performance, or efficiency in an IT 
environment. 
### Problem Scope 
Teams may explore one or more of the following challenge areas: 
• Self-healing systems: Detect and auto-resolve application or infrastructure failures. 
• Predictive operations (AIOps): Use machine learning to forecast performance issues or incidents. 
• Autonomous scaling: Dynamically allocate resources based on demand, cost, or policy 
constraints. 
• Self-securing infrastructure: Identify and remediate security vulnerabilities 
automatically. 
• Autonomous service assurance: Continuously monitor SLAs and adjust configurations to maintain them. 
### Expectations 
• Prototype must show end-to-end autonomy — automated detection, analysis, and 
execution of corrective or optimization actions. 
• Use of AI/ML, rule-based engines, or intelligent agents is highly encouraged. 
• Focus equally on practicality and explainability — how and why the system makes a 
decision. 
• Bonus points for real-time dashboards, cloud integration, or open-source tooling.
